Review 1
Rating: 1/5
While this location is convenient for students, I don’t think the cost (around $1,300/month) is justified given the overall experience. The building has ongoing issues with cleanliness, noise, and security, and management is often unresponsive. Maintenance is especially inconsistent: sometimes requests are handled quickly, but other times it can take multiple follow-ups (I’ve had to do six before) to get a response.

Package handling is also inconvenient. Deliveries are held in the office, meaning you have to wait (sometimes days) for staff to process them, and you can only pick them up during office hours. The Wi-Fi is unreliable, frequently going down, and there is always at least one elevator out of service.
Parking is another major drawback. The underground garage, advertised as heated and secure, costs an additional ~$225/month. However, it has had serious issues, including the garage door being stuck open to the public for months (and still continues to break after being ‘fixed’). Despite this, residents were still charged full price and received no compensation or acknowledgment from management.

Despite their carefully constructed emails and review responses, nothing ever seems to change. Paying top rent and not receiving things you are paying for should not be ok. Actions speak louder than words!! I also find it interesting that you see many reviews here with similar information then some random 5 star raving reviews…

Although the building is relatively new and looks nice at first glance, the construction quality is poor, and it’s rare for everything to be functioning properly at the same time. Overall, there are likely better options that offer more value for the price.

Review 8
Rating: 2/5
Living at Identity Dinkytown has been a frustrating experience from the start. I was part of the first group set to move in on August 1, but due to construction delays, I couldn’t actually move in until December 1. That meant losing an entire semester of college living with my friends, and because I no longer had on-campus housing, I had no other options. They offered gift card subsidies to make up for the delay, but actually getting the money into my account required jumping through endless hoops.
When we finally moved in, the unit was far from what we were promised. The floors were scratched, the shower leaked, and none of us even had shower doors. My roommate’s closet was about 60% of a normal size due to a large pillar inside—something we were never told about until we arrived. On top of that, the overall build quality of the apartment is cheap, from the furniture to the beds to the showers.
The leasing process was equally dishonest. The sales team pressured us into signing by claiming the lease price would increase after a certain deadline. Yet, just two weeks later, they advertised new leases at $200 less per month. When we tried to renegotiate, they refused.
Moving units was another nightmare. We were originally promised 72 hours to move, but just a week before, they changed it to 24 hours, starting from when they “notified” us. That meant dropping everything as soon as we got the message just to move on their timeline.
The management’s lack of care didn’t stop there. When the hot water broke, it took three days to fix. They also randomly recalled all key fobs, causing more unnecessary hassle. Even the hot tub isn’t hot. And for two months straight this winter, the fire alarms went off constantly, often in the middle of the night, making it impossible to get a full night’s sleep.
The only positives are the gym and grills. However, even the gym isn’t maintained—several machines have been broken for weeks, and the clips are faulty.
Overall, this place has been a disappointment. It’s overpriced, poorly managed, and built with cheap materials. I wouldn’t recommend living here.
